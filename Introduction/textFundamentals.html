There are six heading elements. h1 to h6. Each element represents a different level of content in the document

I am h1. The main heading. The first level of content.

In this presentation we want to represent al 6 possible headings with one paragraph in between heading

I am h2. I represent the subheading

You just need to bear in mind a few best practices as you create such structures:

I am h3. I represent th sub-subheading

Preferably you should just use a single h1 per page — this is the top level heading, and all others sit below this in the hierarchy.

I am h4. I represent the sub-sub-subheading

Make sure you use the headings in the correct order in the hierarchy. Don't use h3's to represent subheadings, followed by h2's to represent sub-subheadings — that doesn't make sense and will lead to weird results.

I am h5. I represent the sub-sub-sub-subheading

Of the six heading levels available, you should aim to use no more than three per page, unless you feel it is necessary

I am h6. I represent the sub-sub-sub-sub-subheading

Documents with many levels (i.e. a deep heading hierarchy) become unwieldy and difficult to navigate. On such occasions, it is advisable to spread the content over multiple pages if possible.

The need for Semantics

By definition semantics refers to the meaning of a word, phrase, sentence or text. In HTML we need to make sure we are using the correct elements, giving our content the correct meaning, function, or appearance. In this context the h1 element is also a semantic element, which gives the text it wraps around the role (or meaning) of "a top level heading on your page. Emphasis and Importance

In human language, we often emphasise certain words to alter the meaning of a sentence, and we often want to mark certain words as important or different in some way.

Emphasis

When we want to add emphasis in spoken language, we stress certain words, subtly altering the meaning of what we are saying. In HTML we use the (emphasis) element to mark up such instances.

Strong importance

To emphasize important words, we tend to stress them in spoken language and bold them in written language.In HTML we use the (strong importance) element to mark up such instances

Italic, bold, underline

Elements like this, which only affect presentation and not semantics, are known as presentational elements and should no longer be used, because as we've seen before, semantics is so important to accessibility, SEO, etc.
